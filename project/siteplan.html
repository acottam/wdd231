Site Name

Name: Family Adventures in America's National Parks

Tagline: Simple trip planning for families who love the outdoors.

Why this name: The name clearly communicates that the site focuses on family-friendly planning and covers multiple U.S. National Parks, not just one specific location.

Site Purpose

My website will be a â€œFamily Adventure Plannerâ€ for U.S. National Parks. It will highlight several parks from different regions of the country and explains why each one is great for families. For each park, the site will share kid-friendly hikes and activities, must-see viewpoints, and basic itineraries, and simple planning tips such as when to visit, what to pack, and safety reminders.

The purpose of my site is to give families a simple place to explore park options, see what might fit their interests, and pick up practical tips so they can spend less time stressing over logistics and more time enjoying the experience together. As the course progresses, I plan to use JSON data from public APIs (like the National Park Service API) to dynamically display park information.

Scenarios

  1. As a parent planning our first National Park road trip with kids, I want to compare a few parks by region, driving distance, and kid-friendly activities so I can pick one that fits our time and budget.
  2. As a busy mom or dad, I want a simple 2-3 day itinerary with must-see stops, rest breaks, and drive times so I don't over-schedule my family.
  3. As someone new to hiking, I want to see trail difficulty, distance, elevation, and safety tips so I can choose hikes that match my family's abilities and avoid surprises.
