Essential Planning Tips

Make Your Trip Smooth & Enjoyable

Practical tips and safety reminders for your family adventure

When to Visit

  • Summer (June-August): Peak season, crowded but best weather
  • Spring/Fall: Fewer crowds, mild weather, some facilities may be closed
  • Winter: Least crowded, limited access, special winter activities
  • Book lodging 6-12 months in advance for summer visits

What to Pack

  • Layers: Weather changes quickly in mountains
  • Sun protection: Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
  • Water bottles: Stay hydrated on trails
  • Snacks: Keep kids energized
  • First aid kit: Band-aids, pain reliever, insect repellent
  • Binoculars: Great for wildlife viewing

Safety Reminders

  • Stay on marked trails
  • Keep children within arm's reach near cliffs and water
  • Store food properly to avoid wildlife encounters
  • Check weather forecasts daily
  • Know your limits: Choose trails appropriate for your family's fitness level
  • Carry a map and know where you're going

Making It Fun for Kids

  • Junior Ranger programs: Free activity books at visitor centers
  • Scavenger hunts: Look for specific plants, animals, or landmarks
  • Take breaks: Don't over-schedule
  • Let kids lead: Give them the map or let them choose the next stop
  • Bring a nature journal for drawing and observations
